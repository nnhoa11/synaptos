1.  EXECUTIVE SUMMARY 
SynaptOS is an Agentic AI operating system that is meant to serve fresh-food retail 
supply chains. It connects to the existing POS support and executes the entire price 
determination procedure of perishable stock: reads real-time inventory amounts and expiry 
dates, and decides to alter prices to virtual shelf tags and self-optimizes itself on the 
premise of the later selling rate. 
Its underlying remedy is a commercially acute problem, Vietnamese grocery retailers 
enjoy 2-3 net margin, and waste 15-25 of their fresh commodities to rot. The existing POS 
systems are mere books - they record the buys but contain no information of the expiry 
date, weather variations in demand and the purchasing power of the neighbourhood. Every 
decision to be made on pricing is a manual gut-call, too late and too crude. 
That gut-call is replaced by SynaptOS with a fixed AI reasoner loop that costs $0.015 
a cycle and yields 5-50 dollars of perishable value / decision. The arbitrage gross margin is 
more than 90. 
 
The vison thesis 
SynaptOS converts the biggest cost in the grocery business, spoilage, into the biggest income 
line, agentic AI that no incumbent or global competitor has created in Southeast Asia.

2.  THE PROBLEM 
 
2.1  FRESH FOOD IS A TICKING CLOCK 
The structure of economics of fresh-food retail is aggressive. Even unsold perishable 
products are wasted and their worth cannot be redeemed on a defined time schedule which 
cannot be remedied through any form of management care. A retailer can only do is to 
charge it and at the appropriate time, it will sell the product before it expires. Miss the 
markdown, too small, too late, too big, and the product is rotted or the margin is killed. 
 
Perishable GMV is lost to spoilage by Vietnamese fresh-food retailers at 15 ΓÇô 25% /year. This is 
hundreds of billions of recoverable value being lost annually on margins that are below 3%. to a 
100-store chain that is generating VND 2.1 billion per store per month. 
 
2.2  POS SYSTEMS ARE PASSIVE RECORDERS 
The technology stack that was employed to take care of the vast majority of Vietnamese 
grocery stores was developed with a single purpose in mind, the latter being the monitoring 
of the transactions. POS systems are not smart engines, but books. They are being imported 
with no idea of: 
- 
Perishable SKU expiry dates. 
- 
Live inventory spoilage and depletion. 
- 
Demand varies with weather (during hot seasons, demand on drinks rises, when 
there is a cold front, the demand on hot food moves to the right). 
- 
Variations in purchasing power of the neighbourhood. 
- 
Prices of commodities that affect the behaviour of consumer substitution. 
- 
Purchasing risk and supplier lead times. 
All the pricing decisions, such as when to mark-down, how much, what products are 
done, are all done by a store manager, manually using experience and intuition. The result is 
a failed system of markdowns: intensive 30 - 60% discounts within 24- 48 hours of expiry, 
over which a lesser markdown would have been put in place a priori and sold the product at a 
higher profit. The failure to perform is not a human issue but a systemic issue. Not a single 
manager is able to track 500 SKUs on the flight during 8-hour shift.

3.4. CONTEXTUAL ELASTICITY & DYNAMIC PRICING 
 
Unlike static rule-based systems, SynaptOS segments its pricing 
strategy geographically, demographically, and temporally. This multi-
dimensional elasticity model creates the system's primary Data Moat. The 
agent recognizes that a 20% discount on a Tuesday morning in a residential 
area performs fundamentally differently than the same discount on a Friday 
night at a walking street. 
 
3.4.1. Demographic markdown trajectories 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
- 
Residential zones (light gray line): Prioritizes rapid capital recovery 
via early, aggressive markdowns. 
- 
Premium zones (dark gray line): Defers discounts to the last 
possible hour to protect brand positioning. 
- 
Transit zones (orange line): Maintains flat pricing during the day, 
triggering aggressive flash-sales just before foot traffic drops.

3.4.2. Intraday traffic & temporal execution 
The agent maps its pricing execution to local foot-traffic spikes, 
ensuring markdowns are applied when liquidity is actually present. 
 
 
 
- 
08:00 (suburban morning peak): Traffic spikes as families buy daily 
groceries. The agent holds firm on base prices, refusing to discount 
early, capturing full margin from high-intent buyers. 
- 
12:00 (premium urban lunch rush): Office workers flood Premium 
zones. The agent triggers micro-markdowns (e.g., 5-10%) strictly on 
Ready-to-Eat (RTE) meals nearing expiry, converting perishable risk 
into immediate lunch sales. 
- 
17:00 - 18:00 (suburban post-work rush): The critical clearance 
window for Residential zones. The agent triggers Progressive 
Volume Pricing (e.g., family-pack meat discounts) to clear heavy 
inventory before traffic dies completely at 20:00. 
- 
20:00 (transit / walking street peak): While suburban stores are 
empty, Transit zones reach maximum liquidity. The Agent activates 
Aggressive Flash Sales on snacks and beverages, capturing impulse 
buyers and zeroing out daily inventory.
